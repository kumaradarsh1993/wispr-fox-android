Local history and Android Keystore Audio, transcripts, and keys stay on the phone unless you trigger a provider request.

Built around the overlay.

Floating bubble

Tap to record, tap again to stop, and drag it away from whatever you are doing.

Raw, clean, draft

Keep speech verbatim, lightly clean it, or turn a spoken brief into a polished message.

Auto-paste delivery

Accessibility insertion is the default, with clipboard fallback for stubborn fields.

Phone-first privacy.

Android Keystore

Your API key is stored in the platform keystore, not a plaintext settings file.

On-device history

Recordings and transcripts live locally with configurable retention limits.

No app server

Bring your own provider key. The project does not need an account, proxy, or telemetry endpoint.
